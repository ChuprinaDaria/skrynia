# Legend of Lunula

## Ancient Symbolism

Lunula is one of the oldest Slavic amulets, a symbol of the crescent moon. Archaeologists find lunulas in burials from the 10th-13th centuries, testifying to their importance in our ancestors' lives.

## Meaning in Slavic Culture

### Women's Amulet
Lunula was always considered exclusively a female amulet:
- **Protection of motherhood** – guarded pregnant women and newborns
- **Feminine power** – enhanced feminine energy and beauty
- **Fertility** – helped in conception and childbirth
- **Protection from evil eye** – warded off envy and curses

### Connection with Moon
Moon in Slavic tradition:
- Governs feminine cycles
- Influences fertility of earth and women
- Symbolizes renewal and rebirth
- Illuminates path in darkness

### Crescent Shape
Half-moon looking down (horns up):
- Symbolizes feminine womb
- Receives celestial energy
- Accumulates power for protection
- Reminds of life's cyclical nature

## Combination with Pearls

Pearls in Slavic tradition also held special meaning:

### Pearl Symbolism
- **Purity** – pearls symbolized maiden innocence
- **Tears of joy** – pearl was considered crystallized tear of happiness
- **Feminine wisdom** – pearls granted reason and prudence
- **Protection from illness** – river pearls guarded health

### Power of Water
River pearls carry water's energy:
- Purification and renewal
- Fluidity and adaptation
- Feminine softness and strength
- Connection with ancestral memory

## Wedding Tradition

Necklace with lunula and pearls was often gifted:
- **Young girls** upon coming of age
- **Brides** as wedding amulet
- **Young mothers** for child's protection
- **Between generations** – from grandmother to granddaughter

Such necklace became family heirloom passed from mother to daughter.

## Modern Meaning

Today, necklace with lunula:
- Reminds of feminine power and beauty
- Preserves connection with ancestors
- Helps feel one's femininity
- Serves as powerful amulet for modern woman
- Perfect for wedding ceremonies
