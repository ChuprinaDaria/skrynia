# Power of Garnet - Stone of Warriors and Lovers

## Origin of Name

The name "garnet" comes from Latin "granatus" – grainy, because stone crystals resemble pomegranate seeds. In ancient times, garnet was called "carbuncle" – burning coal.

## Garnet in Slavic Tradition

### Stone of Warriors
Garnet has long been considered warriors' amulet:
- **Protection in battle** – worn as amulet for protection from wounds
- **Courage and valor** – strengthened fighting spirit
- **Blood of life** – red color symbolized blood's power
- **Return home** – believed to help find way back

### Stone of Love
For lovers, garnet had special meaning:
- **Fidelity** – exchanged garnets as sign of faithfulness
- **Passion** – kindled feelings between lovers
- **Protection from betrayal** – darkened if partner was unfaithful
- **Reunion after separation** – helped lovers find each other

## Legends about Garnet

### Fire of Prometheus
According to legend, garnet is frozen drops of Prometheus's blood, who brought fire to people. That's why stone has such powerful energy.

### Stone of Persephone
In Greek mythology, pomegranate (fruit) bound Persephone to underworld. Garnet stone helps find balance between light and darkness.
